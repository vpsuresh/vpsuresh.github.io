Code of Professional Conduct

Interpreters:

  1. Adhere to standards of confidential communication.

  2. Possess the professional skills and knowledge required for the specific interpreting situation.

  3. Conduct themselves in a manner appropriate to the specific interpreting situation.

  4. Demonstrate respect for consumers.

  5. Demonstrate respect for colleagues, interns, and students of the profession.

  6. Maintain ethical business practices.

  7. Engage in professional development.

About Hayes Interpreting Services, LLC

Hayes Interpreting Services, LLC began business operations in December of 1999.  While my primary focus is  medical interpreting, I have a diverse working background in many areas of everyday life. My experience expands to business, education, conference, platform, counseling and private settings such as weddings and funerals.   I have a strong commitment to customer service and I am actively involved with the Deaf community. 

Interpreting involves much more than knowing conversational sign language.  The ability to process information from the source language to the target language requires training and years of hands on work. Our goal is to be sure that the full message is rendered with complete clarity on both sides.

The professional interpreter knows that appearance and conduct on the job are important and second only to the communication connection.

Hayes Interpreting Services, LLC is proud to say we follow the Code of Professional Conduct under the National Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf (RID).

Our dedication to the industry is shown by our membership in the following professional organizations:

  • RID - Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf
  • IAD - Iowa Association of the Deaf
  • ISRID - Iowa State Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf
  • NAD - National Association of the Deaf

Our customers see and know that along with quality interpreting, our business practices are top-notch!
